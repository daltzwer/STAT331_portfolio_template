  • Give one piece of advice to a student just beginning a quarter of STAT 331 that will help them be successful in learning.

One piece of advice I would give to a student just beginning a quarter of STAT 331 is to be patient and TAKE YOUR TIME. I understand just wanting to finish the preview and practice activities as fast as possible. I also understand that sometimes we have those days where we just do not have it in us to read through all the assigned readings and watch the videos; but they really do help. Take it from someone who basically did not have much coding experience, all these assignments and readings are designed to give you a deeper understanding of these concepts, take advantage of them. But just as importantly, be kind to yourself. It can get frustrating sometimes when your code is not working or to not get the grade you want but as long as you are learning, I hope you will find it in yourself to feel good about it. This class was specifically designed with your learning in mind, we are given multiple opportunities to improve on our skills through feedback and revisions, so just try to keep that in mind and the grade will come on its own. Lastly, and this one maybe a little bit more challenging but try not to focus solely on the grade. I understand tying ones idea of success based on the grade we receive, but funnily enough, my biggest take away from this class was to not tie my own self-worth to my grades. I used to strive for 4.0s and feeling pretty terribly every time I fell short. I remember the stress it used to cause me and the affect it had on my health (physical, mental, emotional, you name it). During our classes, we were often reminded by how much our grades should actually be valued and how we should consider how much we can reasonably give for any specific task because giving 100% every time is damn near impossible. I also remember Dr. Theobold showing us a graphic of these cats that said “Comparison is the thief of joy”, which I believe was a quote by Teddy Roosevelt, and that was a real eye opener for me. This class really taught me to value learning and self care, which I find ironic because one wouldn’t think to learn these in a computer programming class. It may not work for everyone but letting go of the idea that you HAVE TO GET AN A every time may allow you to better focus on your growth both as a student and as a person. Good luck to any future STAT 331 students and I hope you walk away with as much as I did in this class. I will forever cherish the lessons I got both in programming and in life.
